How Sweepstakes Software Works in the USA

US-compliant sweepstakes software is built around a promotional framework where players receive virtual currency for free (AMOE — Alternate Method of Entry) and can use sweepstakes coins to play games with the chance to redeem winnings. The software enforces this model at every layer.

The Dual-Currency Wallet System

Every transaction in a sweepstakes casino runs through a dual-wallet — one for Gold Coins (entertainment only) and one for Sweepstakes Coins (redeemable). The software manages purchase flows, bonus awards, and redemption logic to keep operations legally defensible under US promotional law.

AMOE (Alternate Method of Entry)

A core legal requirement: players must be able to obtain sweepstakes entries for free, without purchase. Robust sweepstakes software automates AMOE processing, mail-in entries, and disclosure management so operators stay compliant without manual overhead.

Compliance Requirements for USA Sweepstakes Operators

Compliance is the single biggest reason sweepstakes platforms fail after launch. The most common mistakes — and how the right sweepstakes software prevents them:

  • Incorrect currency structure — Gold Coins and Sweepstakes Coins must be kept separate and clearly disclosed to players at all times.
  • Missing AMOE flows — Every sweepstakes entry mechanism must offer a no-purchase path. Software that doesn't automate this creates legal risk.
  • Weak KYC/AML controls — Player identity verification must be real and documented. Weak checks invite regulatory action and fraud losses.
  • Misleading promotions — Bonus terms, wagering requirements, and redemption conditions must be clear and enforceable within the software itself.
  • No geolocation enforcement — State-level blocking must happen at the session layer, not just at registration.
